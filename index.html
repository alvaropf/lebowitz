What this means: Book value is what's already there. Moat premium is the present value of the competitive advantage (the ROE spread over cost of capital, capitalized forever). Growth premium is what you're paying for earnings to grow beyond zero. If either assumption fails, the multiple unravels.

What this means: Base P/E (1/CoC) is the multiple of a zero-growth business. Moat years is how long the market assumes competitive advantages persist. Growth bet tells you: if the moat lasts N years with G% growth, what P/E results? If you don't believe this (N, G) combination, the price is wrong.
